The day of your USCIS naturalization interview can feel overwhelming. Here is exactly what to bring, what to expect, and how to walk in feeling prepared.

## What to bring with you

Required documents:

- **Interview appointment notice** (USCIS Form I-797C)
- **Green card** (Permanent Resident Card)
- **State-issued photo ID** (driver's license or state ID)
- **All passports** (current and expired) you have used since becoming a permanent resident
- **Re-entry permits and refugee travel documents**, if you have any

Bring originals AND photocopies of everything. The officer may keep copies.

## What happens at the interview

The interview itself usually takes **20 to 30 minutes**. Here is the typical flow:

1. You will be sworn in by the USCIS officer
2. The officer will review your N-400 application with you, asking questions to verify your answers
3. You will take the **English test** (reading one sentence and writing one sentence correctly)
4. You will take the **civics test** (up to 10 questions from the 100-question pool, need to answer 6 correctly to pass)
5. The officer will let you know the result that same day in most cases

## How to dress

Business casual is recommended. You do not need a suit, but avoid t-shirts, shorts, or anything with offensive imagery.

## Common mistakes to avoid

- Arriving late (be there at least 30 minutes early)
- Forgetting any required document
- Giving answers that contradict your N-400 application
- Lying or guessing on questions about your background

## After the interview

You will get one of three results:

> **Granted:** You will be scheduled for the oath ceremony, where you officially become a US citizen.
>
> **Continued:** You need to provide additional documents or retake part of the test. You will receive Form N-14 with instructions.
>
> **Denied:** Your application is denied. You can appeal within 30 days.

Most applicants who have prepared well are **granted on the same day**.
